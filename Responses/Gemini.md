# Executive Summary

Claude 3.5 Sonnet is the inaugural release in the Claude 3.5 model family, engineered to surpass competitor models and the Claude 3 Opus in intelligence while maintaining the cost and speed profile of mid-tier models. The release also introduces "Artifacts," a new feature designed to transform Claude into a collaborative work environment by allowing users to view, edit, and build upon generated content in real-time.

# Key Technical Contributions

- **Reasoning & Coding:** Achieves industry-leading performance on benchmarks such as GPQA (59.4%), MMLU (88.7%), and HumanEval (92.0%).
- **Vision Capabilities:** Outperforms Claude 3 Opus across standard vision benchmarks, particularly in visual math reasoning (67.7%) and chart interpretation (90.8%).
- **Operational Speed:** Operates at twice the speed of the Claude 3 Opus, facilitating complex, multi-step workflows.
- **Agentic Proficiency:** Demonstrated a 64% success rate in internal agentic coding evaluations, doubling the performance of Claude 3 Opus (38%) in troubleshooting and executing code.
- **Efficient Deployment:** Offers a 200K token context window with pricing set at $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens.

# Methodology & Infrastructure

Claude 3.5 Sonnet was developed with a focus on enhancing the tradeoff curve between intelligence, speed, and cost. Safety protocols include rigorous red teaming, resulting in an ASL-2 safety classification, alongside external evaluations by the UK and US Artificial Intelligence Safety Institutes. Furthermore, the developers integrated feedback from child safety experts to refine model classifiers and fine-tuning procedures.

# Limitations & Future Work

The development team plans to complete the Claude 3.5 model family with the release of Claude 3.5 Haiku and Claude 3.5 Opus later in 2026. Future development initiatives include expanding features to support team-based collaboration, integrating with enterprise applications, and implementing "Memory" to retain user preferences and interaction history.
